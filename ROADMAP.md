# ROADMAP.md — the dependency ladder

The honest staged path from the current Mathlib floor up to mono-anabelian reconstruction. Each
rung is marked `NOT-STARTED` / `IN-PROGRESS` / `DONE` with its expected `DEBT` cost. **The bottom
rungs are concrete and near; the top rungs are genuinely multi-year and far.** The distance is not
compressed — saying so is the precondition for ever covering it.

Status as of **Pass 9 (2026-05-30)**. Inventory evidence for every "Mathlib has / lacks X" claim is
in `NOTES.md` (with real declaration names and file paths). Axiom classification convention — and the
anti-drift Reclassification rule — are in `AXIOM_LEDGER.md`.

A structural note on the ladder, because it governs the whole project:

> Rungs L1–L4 are **foundational inputs**: classical theory that is hard to formalize but *not the
> thing we are trying to prove*. They may legitimately be taken as `FOUNDATIONAL` axioms (honest
> boundary markers) or discharged as `DEBT` — either is allowed, because they sit **strictly below**
> the anabelian targets. Rungs R1–R3 (local reconstruction, Neukirch–Uchida, mono-anabelian) are the
> **targets**. Axiomatizing any of *those* — or anything that trivially implies them — is the
> cardinal sin (`CLAUDE.md`, rule 5) and is forbidden. The line between "input we may assume" and
> "theorem we must earn" runs between L4 and R1.

### L4 — Global tools: Chebotarev, global reciprocity, Frobenius   ·   **NOT-STARTED**   ·   DEBT: high

Mathlib **has** number fields, adele rings (`NumberField.AdeleRing`), the Hermite–Minkowski
discriminant bound, and Frobenius in special cases (cyclotomic, finite fields). It **lacks**:
the **Chebotarev density theorem** (ABSENT — 0 hits), **global Artin reciprocity** / the idele-class
reciprocity map (ABSENT), and a general **arithmetic Frobenius at unramified primes** (only special
cases). These are exactly the inputs Neukirch's proof of R2 consumes.
- Depends on: L3, global CFT infrastructure, analytic number theory (for Chebotarev).
- Discharge: `FOUNDATIONAL` and/or `DEBT`; below the targets. Citation: Neukirch, *Algebraic Number
  Theory*, ch. V–VII; Neukirch–Schmidt–Wingberg, *Cohomology of Number Fields*.

## The targets (R1–R3) — must be EARNED, never axiomatized

### R1 — Local reconstruction (mono-anabelian, local)   ·   **NOT-STARTED**   ·   ⚠ multi-year

Recover the arithmetic of a local field `K` from its absolute Galois group `G_K` as a topological
group: residue characteristic `p`, residue cardinality `q`, ramification data, the multiplicative
monoid `O_K \ {0}` up to the relevant equivalence — group-theoretically and (the hard part)
*functorially*. This is the local heart of mono-anabelian geometry.
- Depends on: L1–L3.
- **First genuine anabelian theorems live here.** A realistic *first* target on this rung: recover
  `p` (or `q`) from a group-theoretic invariant of `G_K^ab` via L3. Citation: Mochizuki,
  *A version of the Grothendieck conjecture for p-adic local fields* (1997); Abrashkin.
- **Permitted `FOUNDATIONAL` inputs: L1, L2, L3.** These classical inputs sit below R1 and may be
  taken as boundary markers (logged + classified in `AXIOM_LEDGER.md`). No R-rung is ever a
  permitted `FOUNDATIONAL` input (targets must be earned — see the ledger Reclassification rule).
- `DEBT` budget: this rung's results are **forbidden as axioms**. They are the point.

### R2 — Neukirch–Uchida (number fields)   ·   **NOT-STARTED**   ·   ⚠⚠ major, multi-year on its own

A number field is determined up to isomorphism by its absolute Galois group: every topological
isomorphism `Gal(Q̄/K) ≅ Gal(Q̄/L)` is induced by a field isomorphism `K ≅ L`. The classical
predecessor of everything above. Proof skeleton: characterize decomposition groups group-
theoretically (they are the closed subgroups that are absolute Galois groups of local fields — needs
R1/L1), recover the prime data, then reconstruct via Chebotarev + global reciprocity (L4).
- Depends on: R1, L4 (Chebotarev, global reciprocity), the decomposition-group characterization.
- Citation: Neukirch (1969), Uchida (1976/77); Neukirch–Schmidt–Wingberg, *Cohomology of Number
  Fields*, ch. XII. Function-field analog: Uchida, Pop.
- **Permitted `FOUNDATIONAL` inputs: L1, L2, L3, L4** (notably Chebotarev and global reciprocity).
  **R1 must be a proved theorem here, never assumed** — assuming the local reconstruction R1 to get
  the global R2 would be reclassifying a target as foundational, which the ledger forbids.
- **Forbidden as an axiom.** This is the nearer-but-still-multi-year sub-target, *not* a starting
  point. It presupposes the entire stack L1–L4 + R1.

### R3 — Mono-anabelian reconstruction (the distant end goal)   ·   **NOT-STARTED**   ·   ⚠⚠⚠ distant

The functorial/algorithmic refinement above R2: a group-theoretic *algorithm* that inputs `G_K`
(plus monoid-theoretic data) and outputs the field functorially — "mono-anabelian transport." This
is the forcing construction the companion `iutt` project identified (by kernel-checked self-audit,
twice) as the only thing that converts IUT's "alien copies are non-identifiable" from a *stipulated*
into a *forced* property. The entire purpose of this repository is to earn this, rung by rung.
- Depends on: R2 and the full local mono-anabelian theory (R1 strengthened to functorial form).
- Citation: Mochizuki, *Topics in Absolute Anabelian Geometry* I–III; *Inter-universal Teichmüller
  Theory* I.
- **Permitted `FOUNDATIONAL` inputs: L1, L2, L3, L4. R1 and R2 must be proved theorems, never
  assumed.** Mono-anabelian recovery built on an *assumed* Neukirch–Uchida (R2) or local
  reconstruction (R1) would assert the project's own substance — forbidden.
- **The cardinal-sin axiom.** Stubbing this (or anything implying it) is the one move that would
  void the project. It is named here only to fix the direction of climb, not as anything near.
