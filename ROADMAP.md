# ROADMAP.md ‚Äî the dependency ladder

The honest staged path from the current Mathlib floor up to mono-anabelian reconstruction. Each
rung is marked `NOT-STARTED` / `IN-PROGRESS` / `DONE` with its expected `DEBT` cost. **The bottom
rungs are concrete and near; the top rungs are genuinely multi-year and far.** The distance is not
compressed ‚Äî saying so is the precondition for ever covering it.

Status as of **Pass 17 (2026-05-30)**. Inventory evidence for every "Mathlib has / lacks X" claim is
in `NOTES.md` (with real declaration names and file paths). Axiom classification convention ‚Äî and the
anti-drift Reclassification rule ‚Äî are in `AXIOM_LEDGER.md`.

A structural note on the ladder, because it governs the whole project:

> Rungs L1‚ÄìL4 are **foundational inputs**: classical theory that is hard to formalize but *not the
> thing we are trying to prove*. They may legitimately be taken as `FOUNDATIONAL` axioms (honest
> boundary markers) or discharged as `DEBT` ‚Äî either is allowed, because they sit **strictly below**
> the anabelian targets. Rungs R1‚ÄìR3 (local reconstruction, Neukirch‚ÄìUchida, mono-anabelian) are the
> **targets**. Axiomatizing any of *those* ‚Äî or anything that trivially implies them ‚Äî is the
> cardinal sin (`CLAUDE.md`, rule 5) and is forbidden. The line between "input we may assume" and
> "theorem we must earn" runs between L4 and R1.
