# ROADMAP.md вҖ” the dependency ladder

The honest staged path from the current Mathlib floor up to mono-anabelian reconstruction. Each
rung is marked `NOT-STARTED` / `IN-PROGRESS` / `DONE` with its expected `DEBT` cost. **The bottom
rungs are concrete and near; the top rungs are genuinely multi-year and far.** The distance is not
compressed вҖ” saying so is the precondition for ever covering it.

Status as of **Pass 22 (2026-06-10)**. Inventory evidence for every "Mathlib has / lacks X" claim is
in `NOTES.md` (with real declaration names and file paths). Axiom classification convention вҖ” and the
anti-drift Reclassification rule вҖ” are in `AXIOM_LEDGER.md`.

A structural note on the ladder, because it governs the whole project:

> Rungs L1вҖ“L4 are **foundational inputs**: classical theory that is hard to formalize but *not the
> thing we are trying to prove*. They may legitimately be taken as `FOUNDATIONAL` axioms (honest
> boundary markers) or discharged as `DEBT` вҖ” either is allowed, because they sit **strictly below**
> the anabelian targets. Rungs R1вҖ“R3 (local reconstruction, NeukirchвҖ“Uchida, mono-anabelian) are the
> **targets**. Axiomatizing any of *those* вҖ” or anything that trivially implies them вҖ” is the
> cardinal sin (`CLAUDE.md`, rule 5) and is forbidden. The line between "input we may assume" and
> "theorem we must earn" runs between L4 and R1.
